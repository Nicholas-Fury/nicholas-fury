My Story

From curiosity to transformation

The earliest photo I cherish shows me at age four, sitting at the dinner table with a screwdriver in one hand and a disassembled toy in the other. My mother's exasperated expression tells the rest of the story - that toy was either broken or about to be. In our household, broken things weren't simply replaced; they became opportunities for exploration.

By seven, I'd graduated from toys to household appliances. Our vacuum cleaner, toaster, and eventually my father's record player all experienced my "improvements" with varying degrees of success. What began as childhood curiosity evolved into a systematic approach to problem-solving. Through trial, error, and occasional minor electrical shocks, I developed an intuitive understanding of systems.

My teenage years brought bigger projects: souping up my Nerf arsenal to outrange my friends', repairing my bike chain so it wouldn't slip during paper route deliveries, and finally fixing those temperamental brakes that had failed me on too many downhill adventures. Each success taught me the same lesson: curiosity leads to understanding, understanding enables improvement, and thoughtful improvement transforms possibilities.

This pattern followed me into adulthood and professional life. While others saw established processes as fixed, I recognized the same opportunities for improvement I'd seen in those childhood toys. The stakes were higher, but the approach remained: deconstruct, understand, reimagine, rebuild better.

My most fulfilling work has come from identifying organizational friction points and transforming them into frameworks that scale. I've watched teams reclaim weeks of work through automated systems I designed, seen siloed information transform into enterprise-wide visibility, and helped convert manual, error-prone processes into reliable, efficient operations.

But the true magic moment isn't when I complete a solution - it's when I watch others adopt it, adapt it, and build upon it in ways I never imagined. Like watching a child take their first steps, there's something profoundly satisfying about creating systems that empower others to achieve more with less effort.

I've found that thoughtful automation and AI integration aren't just about efficiency; they create space for human creativity and strategic thinking. When someone shows me how they spend hours on repetitive tasks, I don't just see wasted time - I see opportunity. The same opportunity I saw in that broken toy four decades ago.

Today, I still approach problems with the curiosity of that four-year-old, just with better tools and a broader canvas. Whether mentoring teams, designing operational frameworks, or embedding AI-enhanced workflows into daily practices, I create environments where curiosity flourishes, collaboration thrives, and continuous improvement becomes second nature.

That kid with the screwdriver never really grew up - he just found bigger, more interesting things to fix.

My Guiding Principles

Curiosity First

Understanding the root of a problem always precedes solving it. I ask questions, investigate systems, and seek to understand before acting.

Scale Impact

The most valuable solutions are those that scale beyond initial implementation, creating multiplier effects throughout an organization.

Enable Others

Success isn't measured by what I accomplish alone, but by how I empower others to achieve their goals more effectively.

Simplify Complexity

Complex systems need not be complicated. I transform intricate problems into elegantly simple, maintainable solutions.
